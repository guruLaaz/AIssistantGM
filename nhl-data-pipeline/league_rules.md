### Admin Rights vs. Other GMs

Admins exist solely to facilitate the smooth running of pool activities. Their exclusive rights are:

- Perform certain tasks that Fantrax cannot automate. There are few situations where an admin must manually intervene, but the two most important are: adjusting the remaining claims for GMs involved in a claims trade, and adjusting remaining claims when using Christmas claims (which are not counted normally since they are only temporarily available). In the rare case where Fantrax does not correctly count awarded points, an admin could make the necessary manual corrections.
- One of the admins is responsible for securely holding all GMs' buy-ins and distributing prizes to the winners at the end of the regular season.
- Tracking cumulative standings in order to award the $150 to the cumulative leader (since the start of the cycle) at the end of the season.

### Regular Season + Playoffs Rules

- **Regular season + playoffs:** From Draft Day until the conclusion of the last playoff game.
- **Minimum and maximum players:** 23
- **Minimum forwards:** 12
- **Minimum defensemen:** 6
- **Minimum goalies:** 2
- **Maximum games played for forwards:** 12 × number of regular season games
- **Maximum games played for defensemen:** 6 × number of regular season games
- **Maximum games played for goalies:** number of regular season games
- **Claims available at start of season:** 10
- **Christmas claims:** 2 additional claims are available during the "Holiday period." This period runs from December 18 at 12:00 PM (EST) to January 9 at 11:59 PM. Like regular claims, Christmas claims can be traded. However, Christmas claims can only be traded during the Holiday period.

> Note: Claims used between the end of the draft and the start of the first regular season game following the draft are not counted and will be refunded if necessary. The goal is for everyone to have 10 claims at the start of the first game after the draft.

- **Limit on GM-to-GM transactions (players, claims & picks):** None
- **Draft pick trades:** Only picks from the next draft can be traded.
- **Draft pick trades during the draft:** Picks from the current draft can be traded during the draft.
- **Trade/claims deadline:** Same day as the NHL trade deadline, but at 11:59 PM (instead of the afternoon for the real deadline).
- **Illegal roster:** If you don't respect the minimums/maximums mentioned above or the pool's salary cap, your roster becomes illegal and will not accumulate points until the situation is corrected.
- **GP overage correction:** To correct the fact that Fantrax counts stats on the day you exceed the maximum games played for a given position, 1 point will be deducted per excess game played on that day. This rule applies to all positions.

### Player Eligibility (Draft or Claim)

A player must meet at least one of the following conditions:

1. Have an active NHL contract at the time of being drafted in our draft or claimed during the season.
2. Have previously had an NHL contract (e.g., a GM can pick up Martin Brodeur if he's available in Fantrax).

You therefore cannot pick up a player who has never had an NHL contract. For example, you cannot pick up the 1st overall pick from the last draft if he hasn't signed a contract yet. As soon as he signs an NHL contract, he becomes eligible to be drafted or claimed, provided the claims deadline hasn't passed.

### IR Slot Rules

- You can only place a player who is on the official NHL Injured Reserve list in the IR slot.
- There is only 1 IR slot, so only one cap hit deduction is possible (forwards, defensemen, and goalies included).
- You can place a player in the IR slot at any time (except while his team is playing). Your salary cap space will increase as soon as the player is placed on IR.
- You can use the IR slot as many times as you want. However, each time you place a player on the IR slot, you need to pick up another player, which costs a claim. So in practice, you're limited by the number of claims you have.

**Step-by-step IR example:**

1. **Place injured player in IR slot.** Choose the injured player and send them to the free IR slot. Your salary cap space will increase immediately.
2. **Pick up a replacement from Free Agents.** After placing the injured player in the IR slot, pick up the desired free agent and click "Submit" (not "Next"). This way, you won't be forced to drop a player. Your claim count decreases by 1. *Important: place the player in the IR slot first, otherwise you'll exceed the active player limit.*
3. **When the player returns from injury,** Fantrax does nothing automatically — the player stays in the IR slot without accumulating points. To reactivate them, first drop the replacement player (or any active player), then move the returning player to an active or reserve slot. You can also swap them with another injured player, or drop the IR player. No claim is deducted for this step.
4. **You can swap one injured player for another** in the IR slot as long as you respect the salary cap. However, it's sometimes necessary to first move the active injured player to your regular bench, then swap with the player you want to remove from the IR slot. This way you can save a claim.
5. **When a player's status changes from "Injured Reserve" to "Healthy,"** you have 5 days/periods to remove them from the IR slot. After 5 days, your roster becomes illegal and stops accumulating points until corrected. You must correct the situation before the start of the first game involving one of your players on the 5th day of illegality to avoid losing points.

**Three options to fix an IR situation:**
1. Drop the "Healthy" player still in the IR slot
2. Drop another player to stay under the salary cap
3. Swap in another injured player while staying under the cap

> If you're unable to check your pool for an extended period (vacation, etc.), notify an admin upon your return so they can retroactively apply one of these 3 options to avoid losing points for an extended period. This is at the admins' discretion, but they will be lenient.

**In summary,** the IR slot adds flexibility. It still costs a claim each time it's used (except for start-of-season situations explained above and swaps between your own injured players), but is very useful for stashing long-term injured players.

If, before the draft, you have a keeper eligible to be placed in the IR slot, it's your responsibility to do so before the draft begins. This removes their full cap hit from your payroll, allowing you to benefit during the draft. The IR slot mechanics work for keepers before the draft.

### End-of-Season Tiebreaker

1. The GM with the fewest games played gets priority.
2. If both have the same number of games played, the GM with the most goals scored gets priority.

### Offseason Rules

- **Offseason:** From the conclusion of the last playoff game until the day before the draft.
- Remaining claims from the concluded season can be used during the offseason as soon as it begins.
- GM-to-GM transactions (players, claims & picks) can resume as soon as the offseason begins.
- Unlike the regular season, there is no minimum player requirement for any position during the offseason. However, you must be able to submit a list of 8 keepers before the keeper deadline.

**Player eligibility for offseason claims:**

- A player with an NHL contract must have played a minimum of 10 regular season games in their NHL career to be eligible for a claim during the offseason.
- A player drafted during the offseason (in June/July at the real NHL draft) cannot be claimed. You must wait for our draft in September/October to draft that player.

### Keepers (applicable to seasons 2, 3, 4 & 5 of the cycle)

- **Minimum and maximum keepers:** 8
- A keeper deadline will be set a few days before the draft.
- The draft will then have 15 rounds to fill the remaining 15 positions and reach a roster of 23 players per GM.

### Draft Process

- The draft date is set for the 1st Sunday of October.
- Draft order is determined randomly for the season that starts a cycle. For cycle-starting seasons, the draft uses snake order.
- Draft order for subsequent seasons is based on the previous season's standings.
- Draft order is now straight (non-snake). Example:
  - Round 1: 17th, 16th, 15th, 14th, 13th, 12th, etc.
  - Round 2: 17th, 16th, 15th, 14th, 13th, 12th, etc.
  - Round 3: 17th, 16th, 15th, 14th, 13th, 12th, etc.
- The pick clock during the live draft is 3 minutes, unless a GM is unable to make their selection, in which case the draft can be paused.
- The draft can start before the designated live draft day if a simple majority (>50%) agrees (Remote Draft). This reduces live draft duration. The time per pick will also be determined by majority vote.
- To be eligible for the draft, a GM must have paid their buy-in. Otherwise, their selections will be auto-picked by Fantrax.

### Expansion Draft

- If a new GM joins the pool before the draft, they may draft 8 keepers from the free agent pool after all other GMs have submitted their keeper lists. Additionally, other GMs may protect a 9th player who was on their roster the previous season.
- If the GM joins after the draft, they draft all 23 players from free agents and will have the keeper option for the following year's draft.
- This right can only be exercised once.
- If the GM joins before the draft, their draft position is randomly drawn. If after the draft, their draft position for the next draft is based on the season's results.

---

### "Anti-Pool" Rule

By default, a trade or claim does not require approval from an admin or other GMs. However, if a simple majority (>50%) determines through a democratic vote that a trade or claim is unsportsmanlike, ill-intentioned, or not representative of a trade that could realistically occur in the NHL, the trade or claim can be reversed. Beyond trades, any action that could be harmful to the spirit of healthy and friendly competition that this pool aims to foster can be overturned by a democratic majority vote.

---

### Changes to This Rulebook

Any GM can propose a change to the rules in this Rulebook. For the change to take effect, the proposal must be approved by a simple majority (>50%). Only GMs in place at the time of the vote may vote.
